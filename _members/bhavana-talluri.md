My research interests lie in studying the neuromolecular mechanisms underlying bladder pain, with a focus on bladder physiology, neural circuits, and biochemical signaling using rodent models. I am particularly interested in applying advanced molecular and spatial techniques—including RNAscope in situ hybridization, spatial transcriptomics, TaqMan-based qPCR, and Western blotting—to investigate gene expression, cellular interactions, and associated signaling pathways within the bladder.
Outside the lab, I enjoy playing board games and listening to audiobooks.
